Norway Maple (Acer platanoides)

This tree has a broad canopy that provides shade, reducing urban heat islands and lowering energy use for cooling. It also sequesters significant amounts of carbon over its lifetime.

White Oak (Quercus alba)

Oaks have long lifespans and large biomass, making them excellent carbon sinks. They also support a wide range of wildlife, enhancing biodiversity.

Eastern White Pine (Pinus strobus)

Evergreen pines sequester carbon year-round and help stabilize soil with their extensive root systems, preventing erosion.

Blue Gum Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus)

Moso Bamboo (Phyllostachys edulis)

Bamboo is one of the fastest-growing plants, with some species growing up to a meter per day. It absorbs more carbon dioxide and releases more oxygen than equivalent areas of trees.

Big Bluestem (Andropogon gerardii)

This native prairie grass has deep roots that sequester carbon in the soil, making it an effective carbon sink. It also supports soil health and prevents erosion.

Red Mangrove (Rhizophora mangle)

Mangroves store carbon in their biomass and sediments. They also protect coastal areas from erosion and support marine biodiversity.

Weeping Willow (Salix babylonica)

Silver Birch (Betula pendula)
